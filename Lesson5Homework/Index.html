The Temple of Artemis, located in Ephesus (modern-day Turkey), was one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. Built around 550 BCE, it was dedicated to Artemis, the Greek goddess of the hunt. The temple was renowned for its size, featuring 127 massive marble columns, and housed a large statue of Artemis. Although it was destroyed and rebuilt several times throughout history, most notably by fire in 356 BCE, only a few remnants remain today. It was not only a religious center but also a place of refuge, where people could seek asylum.
